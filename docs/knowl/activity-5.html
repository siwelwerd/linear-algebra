Activity 1.2.2.

Consider the following (equivalent) linear systems.

  1. \begin{align*} x &\,+\,& 2y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 3 \\ -x &\,-\,& y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ 2x &\,+\,& 5y &\,+\,& 3z &\,=\,& 7 \end{align*}
  2. \begin{align*} 2x &\,+\,& 5y &\,+\,& 3z &\,=\,& 7 \\ -x &\,-\,& y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ x &\,+\,& 2y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 3 \end{align*}
  3. \begin{align*} x & & &\,-\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ & & y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ & & y &\,+\,& 2z &\,=\,& 4 \end{align*}
  4. \begin{align*} x &\,+\,& 2y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 3 \\ & & y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ 2x &\,+\,& 5y &\,+\,& 3z &\,=\,& 7 \end{align*}
  5. \begin{align*} x & & &\,-\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ & & y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ & & & & z &\,=\,& 3 \end{align*}
  6. \begin{align*} x &\,+\,& 2y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 3 \\ & & y &\,+\,& z &\,=\,& 1 \\ & & y &\,+\,& 2z &\,=\,& 4 \end{align*}

Rank the six linear systems from most complicated to simplest.
